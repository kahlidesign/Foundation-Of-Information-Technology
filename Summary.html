FOUNDATION OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

Summary of Module 1 ; Objectives 1 , 2 & 3

Objective 1

Information Technology (IT) encompasses hardware, software, the internet, and telecommunications, shaping how data is processed and shared. Related fields include computing, computer science, software engineering, computer engineering, and information systems, each with distinct but interconnected roles. Professional organizations like IEEE, BCS, ACM, and AIS set industry standards and advance research.

Objective 2

IT history spans supercomputers to mobile devices, early programming to AI-driven software, and ARPANET to modern 5G connectivity. Telecommunications evolved from the telegraph to satellite networks, improving global communication.

Objective 3

Data refers to raw, unprocessed facts, while information is structured data with meaning. Knowledge is the application of information for decision-making. IT enables businesses, communication, and automation but faces challenges like cybersecurity, privacy, and ethical considerations.

Difference Among Data, Information & Knowledge

Aspect Data Information Knowledge
Definition Raw facts or figures without context Processed data providing meaning Applied understanding that aids decision making
Format Numbers, text, symbols Reports, tables, summaries Concepts, insights, expertise
Use Stored but not useful on its own Helps in analysis and decision-making Leads to effective actioms and predictions
Example A list of numbers A report summarizing trends from those numbers A strategy based on the report's findings
